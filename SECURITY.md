# Security Policy

We take the security of NOVA Cyber & Technology products seriously and welcome responsible disclosure from the security research community.

---

## Supported Products

| Project | Status |
|---|---|
| NovaCont | ✅ Supported |
| NovaCont Lite | ✅ Supported |
| NovaCTI | ✅ Supported |
| Official Website | ✅ Supported |

---

## Reporting a Vulnerability

**Please do not disclose vulnerabilities publicly before they've been reviewed and addressed.**

Report privately to: **support@novatechnology.app**

Include as much of the following as you can:

- A clear description of the vulnerability
- Steps to reproduce
- Expected vs. actual behavior
- A proof of concept (PoC), if available
- Screenshots or logs, if applicable
- Potential impact

Reports with clear reproduction steps get resolved faster.

> Reporting a smart contract issue? Please include the affected contract address/network (mainnet, Sepolia testnet, or TON) and, if possible, whether user funds are at risk.

---

## Response Targets

| Stage | Target |
|---|---|
| Initial acknowledgement | Within 72 hours |
| Triage & severity assessment | Within 7 days |
| Fix or mitigation for critical/high issues | As fast as responsibly possible - smart contract and fund-affecting issues are treated as top priority |

We'll keep you updated as we work through remediation. These are targets, not guarantees - response time can vary with severity and complexity.

---

## Severity & Priority

We generally prioritize by potential impact:

- **Critical** - loss or theft of user funds, contract logic bypass, full compromise of a service
- **High** - privilege escalation, authentication bypass, significant data exposure
- **Medium / Low** - limited-impact bugs, issues requiring unusual preconditions

Critical and high-severity smart contract findings are always treated as top priority, given what's at stake for users.

---

## Safe Harbor

If you make a good-faith effort to comply with this policy while researching and reporting a vulnerability, we will not pursue or support legal action against you for that research. This safe harbor applies only to testing conducted in line with the rules below. Testing outside those bounds isn't covered.

---

## Responsible Disclosure Guidelines

Please:

- Avoid disrupting production services
- Never access, modify, or exfiltrate user data beyond what's needed to demonstrate the issue
- Don't perform denial-of-service (DoS) testing
- Don't publicly disclose a vulnerability before it's resolved
- Only test systems you're authorized to access

---

## Scope

**In scope:**
- NovaCont (smart contracts and platform)
- NovaCont Lite
- NovaCTI
- Official websites and public APIs
- Official GitHub repositories

**Out of scope:**
- Third-party services and dependencies we don't control
- Social engineering or phishing attempts against team members or users
- Physical security
- Reports generated purely by automated scanners without a demonstrated, exploitable issue
